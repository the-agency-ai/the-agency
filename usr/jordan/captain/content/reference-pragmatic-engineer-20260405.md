## Business Model

**Pure subscription.** No ads in newsletter. No sponsors. No affiliates. No courses. No brand deals. Explicitly refuses all of these.

- Free tier: ~1 full article/month + partial previews
- Paid tier: Full Tuesday deep-dives + Thursday "The Pulse" + podcast. ~4x content volume.
- Pricing: $15/mo or $150/yr. Regional pricing (lower rates outside US/Western Europe). Student discounts. Team subscriptions.
- Revenue: Publicly stated $1.5M+/yr ARR (at ~400K subs). At 1M+ subs, estimated $2-3M+ ARR.

**Podcast:** Launched Oct 2024. Sponsors only (not paywalled). 2-3 episodes/month. 10M+ downloads by end 2025.

**Book:** *The Software Engineer's Guidebook* (2023). 4 years to write. Amazon #1. 6 languages. Hardcover + audiobook. Book readers → newsletter subscribers.

**Shut down:** Talent Collective and Job Board (ran 2.5 years, shut Apr 2024 — distraction).

## Team

2 people. Gergely + Elin Nilsson (Tech Industry Researcher, hired early 2024, ex-Spotify). 100+ issues/year.

## Content Strategy

**Two-cadence structure:**
- Tuesday: evergreen educational (systems design, career, engineering management)
- Thursday: timely industry pulse (layoffs, Big Tech moves, startup trends)

**Credibility-first:** Writes as operator (Uber, Skype, Skyscanner), not commentator. Deep dives are research-intensive — interviews practitioners, doesn't recycle press releases.

**Growth channels:** Blog SEO → newsletter. X → newsletter. Substack recommendations (70% of growth). Speaking → newsletter. Podcast → newsletter. Book → newsletter. All roads lead to the newsletter.

**Paywalling:** "First half free" model. Creates enough value to hook, enough friction to convert.

## Key Credibility Moments

1. **2022 tech layoff wave** — live-blogged and reported in real time. The Pulse became the go-to primary source. Massive subscriber spike.
2. **DevTernity exposé** — exposed fake female speaker profiles. Got wide tech press coverage. Built investigative reputation.
3. **Consistent prediction record** on Big Tech restructuring.

## Why It Works

Single sentence: One person with genuine operator credibility, writing for a high-earning audience ($150K-$400K+ comp), at a trivially expensable price point, with no sponsor dependency so editorial independence is real, which reinforces trust, which drives word-of-mouth, at near-zero operating cost.
